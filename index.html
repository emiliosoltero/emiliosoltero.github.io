Emilio Soltero

Second year mechanical engineering student at the University of Bath

About Myself

I discovered my passion for Engineering when I was first introduced to the internal workings of a combustion engine. The concept of a system working at perfect timings and the idea of controlling and modifying it fascinated me. As a child, I also enjoyed building Legos and Maths, leading me to participate in many regional and national Maths competitions in my home country of Mexico. Living in different countries, I've learnt about different cultures and languages, and currently speak English, Spanish and French.

Interests and hobbies

I particularly enjoy watching engineering and history documentaries, reading, and working on personal projects (whether it be CAD design or coding). I also enjoy exercising, playing chess, and language learning.

Extracurricular Activities

I have taken part in various activities, including working part-time as staff at my university's Students Union Bar, joining Bath Team Rocket in my first year, and Team Bath Racing Electric in my second year.

Personal Projects

A quick overview of my projects, which mostly include 3D models on Autodesk Inventor and engineering drawings.

Traffic Cone Unloader

A team assignment to conceptualise, design and build a model traffic cone unloader controlled via pure mechanisms.
